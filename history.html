Our History

A decade of innovation, growth, and community building

The ECHO Journey

2016

The Foundation

ECHO Club was founded by a group of passionate electronics and computing enthusiasts who wanted to create a space for students to explore technology beyond the classroom. Starting with just 12 members, we built our first project: an Arduino-based weather station.

2017

First Electronics Lab

We secured our first dedicated workspace with soldering stations, oscilloscopes, and 3D printers. Membership grew to 75 students as word spread about our hands-on approach to learning.

2018

Inaugural Tech Summit

Launched our first annual Tech Summit, bringing together students from 15 universities. Over 200 participants attended workshops on IoT, robotics, and embedded systems.

2019

Industry Partnerships

Established partnerships with leading tech companies for internship programs and sponsored projects. Our members started winning regional and national hackathons.

2020

Digital Transformation

Pivoted to virtual events during the pandemic, launching our online learning platform with 30+ recorded workshops. Created remote collaboration tools for distributed project teams.

2021

Community Expansion

Reached 300 active members. Launched mentorship program pairing students with alumni in tech industry. Started monthly "Build Nights" for collaborative projects.

2022

Research Initiatives

Members published first research paper on sustainable electronics. Partnered with local schools for STEM outreach programs reaching 500+ K-12 students.

2023

New Innovation Center

Moved to a 2,000 sq ft innovation center with dedicated zones for PCB fabrication, 3D printing, and VR development. Hosted international hackathon with 50+ teams.

2024

Alumni Network Launch

Formalized alumni network with 200+ members working at top tech companies. Established scholarship fund supporting 10 students annually.

2026

Today & Beyond

Now with 500+ active members, 50+ events per year, and a thriving community spanning students, faculty, and industry partners. We continue to push the boundaries of what students can achieve in technology.
