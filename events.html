AI Workshop Series Registration Open

📅 February 15, 2026 📍 Tech Lab, Room 301 ⏰ 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Join our comprehensive 4-week AI workshop covering machine learning fundamentals, neural networks, natural language processing, and computer vision. No prior ML experience needed — we start from scratch. Each session includes theory, hands-on coding, and take-home exercises. Participants will build and deploy their own AI model by the end of the series. Certificates provided upon completion.

ECHO Team Wins National Hackathon 2025

📅 January 28, 2026 🏆 National Level Achievement

Our team of four students — Aarav Reddy, Sneha Kumari, Vikram Patel, and Divya Sharma — secured 1st place at the National Engineering Hackathon 2025 held at IIT Hyderabad. Their winning project, "AquaGuard IoT", is an intelligent water quality monitoring system for rural communities that uses low-cost sensors and cellular connectivity to detect contamination in real-time.

Tech Talk: IoT in Smart Cities

📅 February 20, 2026 📍 Auditorium A 🎤 Dr. Priya Sharma, IIT Delhi

Dr. Priya Sharma, a leading researcher in urban IoT infrastructure, will share her insights on how Internet of Things technology is transforming cities across India. Topics include smart traffic management, environmental monitoring, waste management automation, and the challenges of deploying IoT at urban scale. Open to all students and faculty. Q&A session follows the lecture.

Lab Safety Guidelines Update

📅 February 10, 2026 📋 Circular

Updated safety protocols for electronics lab usage are now in effect. Key changes include mandatory safety goggles when soldering, new fire extinguisher locations, updated first-aid procedures, and mandatory safety orientation for all new members before lab access is granted. Please review the full guidelines document available at the lab entrance.

PCB Design Workshop

📅 March 5, 2026 📍 Electronics Lab ⏰ 5:00 PM - 7:30 PM

Learn professional PCB design techniques using KiCad, the industry-standard open-source EDA tool. This hands-on workshop covers schematic capture, footprint assignment, PCB layout, design rule checks, and generating Gerber files for manufacturing. Each participant will design their own PCB during the session. Laptops required — KiCad installation instructions will be sent before the event.
